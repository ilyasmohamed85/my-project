Where It All Began


Barbering is more than a cut — it’s a culture, a tradition, a craft passed down through centuries.


Ancient Roots: Barbering can be traced back as far as 5000 B.C. In ancient Egypt, barbers used sharpened flint and seashells to groom their clients. In ancient Rome, barbershops (called tonstrina) were central hubs for news and social connection.


Middle Ages: Barbers were known as barber-surgeons. They not only cut hair and shaved beards, but also performed surgeries, bloodletting, and even tooth extractions. That’s where the red and white barber pole comes from — red for blood, white for bandages.


Modern Era: By the 18th and 19th centuries, barbering evolved into a specialized grooming profession. In America and Europe, barbershops became cultural institutions — places to talk, relax, and stay sharp.


Today: The modern barber blends tradition with style — offering fades, shaves, line-ups, and grooming with flair and precision. It’s not just about how you look — it’s how you feel when you walk out of that chair.
